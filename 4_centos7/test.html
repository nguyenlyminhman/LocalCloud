This is the example (and the data) of a real Hackuity client. Our client has 3 people fully dedicated to vulnerability management. Thanks to Hackuity, this customer saves 40% in exploitation and remediation time.

This saves $125,000 per year, as the people in charge of the VM practice can be assigned to other tasks. Indirectly, Hackuity even saves costs associated with the recruiting process - since engineers are no longer used on redundant, uninteresting tasks, they are less likely to leave their position and the organization.

Worforce time saving (hours) / year

4 983 hours are estimated to be need to prevent, detect and remediate vulnerabilities by this organization team. With Hackuity, it goes down to 1 993 hours. That's a 40% cut. And so much more hours left to focus on remediation and advanced tasks.

Workforce re-allocation ($) cost / year

On the financial part, taking in consideration an IT security hourly rate is approximately of $62.50. With Hackuity's digitalization of vulnerability management, this organization saves $125 000.

Reduction of the number of Critical Vulnerabilities to manage

The following data is derived from extrapolated data from 50 different Hackuity customers, allowing us to calculate an average reduction rate of critical vulnerabilities among our customers. We found that our True Risk Score (TRS) usage provides an average x38 reduction of the number of critical vulnerabilities that need to be remediated immediately inside the organizations.

CVSS Score - Number of Critical Vulnerabilities to fix immediately using the classic CVSS score

Out of 45 300 vulnerabilities found, 4 500 are considered critical by the CVSS score - making remediation practice almost impossible to perform.

TRS Score - Number of Critical Vulnerabilities to fix immediately using the Hackuity True Risk Score

Out of 45 300 vulnerabilities found, 119 are considered critical by the TRS score - finally making the practice of remediation possible.

The TRS score takes into account the classic vulnerability score, the actual risk level related to the exploitation of the vulnerability and the business context of the assets within the organization. By taking into account a global context, the TRS score allows you to focus on truly critical vulnerabilities: focus on what really matters.
